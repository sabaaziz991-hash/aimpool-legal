AimPool Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 9, 2026

AimPool: Billiards Shot Coach ("AimPool") is designed to analyze pool-table photos on your device.

Summary

  • AimPool processes imported or captured table photos on-device.
  • AimPool does not upload your photos to a server.
  • AimPool does not require accounts.
  • AimPool does not include analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.
  • Purchases are handled by Apple through StoreKit. AimPool never sees payment-card or billing details.
  • The built-in sample table is free; camera and library photo analysis require AimPool Premium.
  • Dataset and benchmark exports are local, user-initiated, and opt-in.

Photos and camera

AimPool lets you choose a photo from your library or capture a table photo with the camera. The image supports table calibration, ball review, and shot recommendations inside the app. Photo analysis runs on-device, and AimPool does not upload those photos.

If you use dataset-export features, AimPool writes the selected source image and annotation metadata to local files only when you choose an export action.

Purchases

AimPool Premium is a one-time non-consumable purchase handled by Apple StoreKit. Apple processes payment and billing information. AimPool receives StoreKit product and entitlement status but does not receive payment-card numbers or Apple account billing details.

Free sample and premium access

AimPool's free mode lets you explore the built-in sample table. Camera capture, photo-library import, and analysis of your own table photos require AimPool Premium. The current product model does not use a free-analysis ledger to meter own-photo access.

Optional downloads

AimPool's normal photo-analysis flow does not contact an AimPool server. Outbound network paths are Apple StoreKit and optional, user-entered HTTPS downloads in Settings developer tools for detector or shot-policy packages. Those downloads happen only when you enter a URL and start them.

Analytics and tracking

AimPool does not include analytics SDKs, ad SDKs, tracking SDKs, an App Tracking Transparency prompt, or IDFA access. AimPool does not sell personal data.

Contact

For privacy questions or support, visit the AimPool support page.
